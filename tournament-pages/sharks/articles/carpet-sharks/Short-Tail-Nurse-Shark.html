Short-Tail Nurse Shark

Short-tail nurse sharks possess exceptionally rough skin covered in enlarged dermal denticles that helps protect them from abrasive coral surfaces where they often rest. Unlike their more common nurse shark relatives, these nocturnal predators have a noticeably shorter caudal fin and are primarily found in the western Pacific Ocean, where they use their powerful suction feeding technique to extract prey from tight crevices.
