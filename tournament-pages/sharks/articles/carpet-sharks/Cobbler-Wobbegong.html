Cobbler Wobbegong

Cobbler wobbegongs have distinctive honeycomb-like patterns across their bodies and are named after the patchwork appearance of a cobbler's work, providing exceptional camouflage in their reef habitats. These Australian ambush predators possess an unusually wide mouth that can open larger than their head circumference, allowing them to swallow surprisingly large prey whole after patiently waiting motionless for hours.
