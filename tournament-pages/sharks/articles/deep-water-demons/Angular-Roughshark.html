Angular roughsharks have an unmistakable humpbacked appearance with a flattened head and rough, thorn-like scales covering their distinctive triangular bodies. These unusual sharks excrete a foul-smelling oily substance from their skin that gives them their Portuguese name peixe porco (pig fish) and makes them unpalatable to predators in the deep waters of the eastern Atlantic.
