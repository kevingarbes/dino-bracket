Shark Overview

Ninja lanternsharks are named for their stealth-like black coloration that helps them remain nearly invisible in their deep-sea habitat. Discovered only in 2010, these small sharks (reaching just 18 inches in length) produce a faint bioluminescent glow from specialized organs called photophores that helps them camouflage against the dim light filtering from above.
