Japanese Sawshark

Japanese sawsharks possess an elongated, toothed snout that resembles a saw, which they swing from side to side to stun and impale prey on the ocean floor. Unlike the more well-known sawfish, these rare bottom-dwelling sharks have a pair of unique barbels halfway along their snouts that act as sensory organs to detect hidden prey in sediment and seagrass beds.
