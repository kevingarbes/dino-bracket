Hepatica

Round-lobed hepatica (Anemone americana) is a petite spring wildflower named for its three-lobed leaves that resemble a human liver (“hepatic” means liver-like). Under old medical doctrine of signatures, this resemblance led people to believe hepatica could treat liver ailments. It blooms very early, often while snow still lingers, with starry flowers that can be white, pink, lavender, or deep purple. The buds and stems are covered in fine hairs that provide insulation against frosty nights. Once the trees leaf out and shade the forest, hepatica’s flowers are long gone, and its leaves, having overwintered from the previous year, soon get replaced by fresh ones that will persist into next spring.
