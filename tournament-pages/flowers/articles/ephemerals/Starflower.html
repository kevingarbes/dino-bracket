Starflower

Starflower (Trientalis borealis) is a delicate understory plant named for its starry white flowers. Uniquely, it’s a “plant of sevens”—often bearing seven pointed petals, seven stamens, and even seven leaves in a whorl. Blooming in late spring in cool forests, starflowers spread slowly by runners to form a thin carpet. They have no nectar and depend on wind pollination, so their charm is purely visual. By midsummer, they slip back into dormancy, their constellation of stars gone until the following spring.
