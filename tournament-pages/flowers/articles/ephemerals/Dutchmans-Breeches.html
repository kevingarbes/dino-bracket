Ephemerals Division - #2 Seed

Dutchman’s Breeches

Dutchman’s breeches (Dicentra cucullaria) is a tiny woodland wildflower whose white blooms look like little pantaloons hung upside-down on the line. The unique shape isn’t just whimsical; it shelters pollen from rain and only a specific pollinator can reach its nectar. In fact, only the female bumblebee with a long enough tongue can access the nectar deep in the breeches’ spur, pollinating the flower in the process. After its brief early-spring flowering, the plant disappears by summer, living on in its underground tubers.
