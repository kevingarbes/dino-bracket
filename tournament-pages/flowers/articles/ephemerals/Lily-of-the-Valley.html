Ephemerals Division - #7 Seed

Lily-of-the-Valley

Lily-of-the-valley (Convallaria majalis) is a shade-loving perennial often called an ephemeral because its foliage tends to die back as summer heat arrives. In mid-spring it produces sprays of dainty bell-shaped white flowers famous for an intoxicating, sweet perfume. Despite its innocent appearance, the entire plant is poisonous - however, its fragrance has made it beloved for centuries (it’s a traditional wedding bouquet flower, chosen by brides like Princess Kate Middleton). In folklore, lily-of-the-valley is said to spring from the tears of the Virgin Mary, symbolizing sweetness and the return of happiness.
