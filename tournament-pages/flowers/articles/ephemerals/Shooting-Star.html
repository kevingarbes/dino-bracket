Ephemerals Division - #12 Seed

Shooting Star

Shooting star (Dodecatheon meadia) is a prairie and woodland ephemeral whose unusual flowers resemble tiny shooting stars falling to earth. In late spring it sends up leafless stems with multiple pink or white reflexed-petal flowers that point backwards while the yellow center points downwards. Bumblebees pollinate these by a method called “buzz pollination,” shaking the flowers to release pollen. After a brief bloom period, the whole plant disappears by midsummer. Gardeners prize shooting stars in wildflower gardens, and it’s interestingly related to primroses (and sometimes classified in genus Primula now), even though it looks so different.
