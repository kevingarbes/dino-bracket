Bloodroot

Bloodroot (Sanguinaria canadensis) is one of the first wildflowers of spring, often blooming through leaf litter in March with pristine white petals around a yellow center. Each flower lasts only a day or two, and at night or in cold rain the petals close up protectively?. It gets its name from its underground rhizome, which oozes red sap when cut. Indigenous peoples used bloodroot’s red-orange juice as a natural dye and in medicinal salves, though the plant is toxic if ingested.
