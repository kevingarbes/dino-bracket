Ephemerals Division - #8 Seed

Crocus

Crocuses are small spring-blooming corms that are often the first flowers to poke through snow, sometimes blooming as early as February. A patch of purple, gold, and white crocus cups opening in late winter sun is a welcome sign of spring’s approach. Interestingly, the spice saffron comes from a type of crocus (Crocus sativus); each flower of that crocus yields only three tiny saffron threads (stigmas), which helps explain why saffron is one of the world’s most expensive spices. After blooming, crocus grass-like leaves fatten the corm for a few weeks before the plants retreat underground for the rest of the year.
