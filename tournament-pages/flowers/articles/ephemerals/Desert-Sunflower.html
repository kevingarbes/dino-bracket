Desert Sunflower

Desert sunflower (Geraea canescens), also called desert gold, is an annual ephemeral that bursts into bloom after rare heavy rains in the Southwest deserts. In places like Death Valley, this plant’s seeds can lie dormant for years until conditions are just right—then millions of bright yellow daisies carpet the desert floor in a “superbloom,” astonishing those who think of deserts as barren. Each bloom is fleeting under the harsh sun, and by late spring the entire population dries up and virtually disappears, surviving only as seeds waiting for the next rain miracle. This boom-and-bust life strategy allows desert ephemerals like the desert sunflower to flourish despite an extreme environment.
