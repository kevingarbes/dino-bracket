Ephemerals Division - #9 Seed

Snowdrop

Snowdrops (Galanthus nivalis) live up to their name by emerging through melting snow with nodding white flowers, often in late winter. These petite bulbs have specialized “anti-freeze” proteins that let them survive freezing nights. Snowdrops have been cherished since Victorian times - there’s even a word “galanthophile” for a collector obsessed with snowdrop varieties. One legend says that when Adam and Eve were cast out of the Garden of Eden into winter, an angel turned falling snowflakes into snowdrop flowers to give them hope; thus, snowdrops symbolize hope and rebirth, a fitting message delivered each year as they bloom and fade in the span of a few weeks.
