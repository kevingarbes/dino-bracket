Sand Verbena

Desert sand verbena (Abronia villosa) is a low-growing sand dune wildflower that blooms quickly after desert winter rains, forming fragrant clusters of pinkish-purple flowers. At dusk, its sweet scent intensifies and can perfume the night air, luring moths. Large “fields” of sand verbena sometimes bloom in desert valleys during superblooms, painting the sand pastel pink. But like many desert ephemerals, their show is short-lived—within weeks of flowering and setting seed, the plants wither away. The seeds remain buried in the sand, enduring possibly years of drought until the next opportunity when rain and conditions align for another round of dazzling but ephemeral beauty.
