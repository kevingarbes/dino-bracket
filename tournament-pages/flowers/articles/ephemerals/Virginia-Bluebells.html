Virginia Bluebells

Virginia bluebells (Mertensia virginica) carpet moist woodlands in early spring with their nodding clusters of blooms that start pink and turn sky-blue. These ephemeral perennials emerge quickly and vanish by early summer. A threatened species in some areas due to habitat loss?, bluebells are beloved by gardeners and bumblebees alike - their tubular blossoms provide a vital nectar source for queen bumblebees that happen to be out early foraging.
