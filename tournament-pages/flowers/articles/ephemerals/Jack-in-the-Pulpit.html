Jack-in-the-Pulpit

Jack-in-the-pulpit (Arisaema triphyllum) is a curious woodland plant consisting of a green or purple-striped hood (the “pulpit”) sheltering a club-like spadix (“Jack”) inside. It appears in spring and often goes dormant by late summer. Fascinatingly, these plants can change their sex from year to year: a smaller or stressed plant produces only male flowers (Jack stays “Jack”), but a vigorous plant with enough energy will produce female flowers (becoming “Jill-in-the-pulpit” with berries after bloom). This sequential hermaphroditism in Jack-in-the-pulpits means one year’s “Jack” might be next year’s “Jill,” depending on conditions - a unique strategy in the plant kingdom.
