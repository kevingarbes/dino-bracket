Dahlia

Dahlias are tuberous-rooted perennials famed for their spectacular flower heads, which can range from tiny pompoms to huge “dinner plate” dahlias nearly a foot wide. Native to Mexico (and its national flower), dahlias were cultivated by the Aztecs for food and ornament. Today they’re prized in flower shows - for example, the annual San Francisco Dahlia Show displays the city’s official flower, showcasing the incredible spectrum of colors and forms this plant can produce.

Dahlias are tuberous-rooted perennials famed for their spectacular flower heads, which can range from tiny pompoms to huge “dinner plate” dahlias nearly a foot wide.
