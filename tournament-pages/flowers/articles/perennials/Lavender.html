Flower Overview

Lavender (Lavandula) is a shrubby perennial herb cherished for its soothing fragrance and spikes of purple flowers. Long used in aromatherapy and medicine, lavender oil has natural antiseptic properties - it’s said that Victorian lavender growers avoided cholera outbreaks due to constant contact with the oil. Even pests dislike it: the aroma repels mosquitoes and moths, making lavender a lovely and practical garden addition.
