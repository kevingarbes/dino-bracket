Lily

True lilies (Lilium species) grow from bulbs and bear large, often freckled, trumpet-shaped flowers—like the Easter lily or the fragrant Oriental lilies. Each lily bulb can produce new “baby” bulbs underground, so a clump can multiply over time. Lilies are so revered that the city of Florence, Italy, uses a red lily (iris) as its emblem, and the white Madonna lily has symbolized purity in art and literature for centuries.
