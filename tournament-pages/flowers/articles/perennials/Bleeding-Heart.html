Bleeding Heart

Old-fashioned bleeding heart (Dicentra spectabilis) adorns shaded gardens with dangling pink heart-shaped flowers each spring. The plant’s romantic blooms have inspired folklore: one tale imagines a prince (the flower’s “heart”) trying to woo a maiden with gifts (the inner petals resemble a slipper and a dangled earring) before he tragically “bleeds” for her love - thus the bleeding heart. After blooming, the ferny foliage often goes dormant in summer heat, a natural lifecycle quirk of this ephemeral perennial.

Flower Overview

Old-fashioned bleeding heart (Dicentra spectabilis) adorns shaded gardens with dangling pink heart-shaped flowers each spring. The plant’s romantic blooms have inspired folklore: one tale imagines a prince (the flower’s “heart”) trying to woo a maiden with gifts (the inner petals resemble a slipper and a dangled earring) before he tragically “bleeds” for her love - thus the bleeding heart. After blooming, the ferny foliage often goes dormant in summer heat, a natural lifecycle quirk of this ephemeral perennial.
