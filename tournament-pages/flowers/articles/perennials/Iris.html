Iris

Irises are named after the Greek goddess of the rainbow for their variety of colors, from deep purple and blue to yellow and white. The bearded iris, common in U.S. gardens, has a unique “landing pad” of fuzzy hairs that guides pollinators. Orris root (from certain iris rhizomes) has been used in perfumery and even to flavor gin, demonstrating this elegant flower’s practical side.

Flower Overview

Irises are named after the Greek goddess of the rainbow for their variety of colors, from deep purple and blue to yellow and white. Orris root (from certain iris rhizomes) has been used in perfumery and even to flavor gin, demonstrating this elegant flower’s practical side.
