Daylily

Daylilies (Hemerocallis) are ultra-tough perennials whose lily-like blooms last only one day each—but each plant produces many buds for an extended show. Often called the “perfect perennial,” common orange daylilies escaped gardens and now naturalize along roadsides across the U.S. (sometimes nicknamed “ditch lilies”). Hybridizers have developed thousands of varieties, and some modern daylilies even rebloom later in the season.
