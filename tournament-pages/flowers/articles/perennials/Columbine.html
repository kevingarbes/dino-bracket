Perennials Division - #14 Seed

Columbine

Columbine (Aquilegia) is a woodland perennial with elegant, spurred flowers that often appear bi-colored (for example, red and yellow in the wild Eastern columbine). Colorado’s state flower is the blue Rocky Mountain columbine, so cherished that picking one in the wild was made illegal. The genus name comes from Aquila, meaning eagle, because the flower’s spurs resemble an eagle’s talons, and the common name “columbine” comes from columba (dove) - the inverted blooms were thought to look like five doves clustering together.

Flower Overview

Columbine (Aquilegia) is a woodland perennial with elegant, spurred flowers that often appear bi-colored (for example, red and yellow in the wild Eastern columbine). Colorado’s state flower is the blue Rocky Mountain columbine, so cherished that picking one in the wild was made illegal. The genus name comes from Aquila, meaning eagle, because the flower’s spurs resemble an eagle’s talons, and the common name “columbine” comes from columba (dove) - the inverted blooms were thought to look like five doves clustering together.
