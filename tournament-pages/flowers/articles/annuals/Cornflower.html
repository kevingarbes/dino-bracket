Cornflower

Worn in the buttonholes of bachelors in love according to folklore, cornflowers are also a symbol of remembrance (it’s the national flower of Estonia) and have been used to add a blue tint to teas and vinegars.

Flower Overview

Cornflower (Centaurea cyanus), or bachelor’s button, is a classic wildflower bearing crisp blue blossoms that were once common in European grain fields (hence the name). Worn in the buttonholes of bachelors in love according to folklore, cornflowers are also a symbol of remembrance (it’s the national flower of Estonia) and have been used to add a blue tint to teas and vinegars.
