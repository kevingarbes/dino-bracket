Flower Overview

The common sunflower (Helianthus annuus) can tower over a garden—often 6–10 feet tall—with huge golden heads that turn toward the sun. Sunflowers are not only ornamental; they are also grown as a major crop for seeds and oil, and a single sunflower head is composed of hundreds of tiny individual florets full of nectar for bees.
