Flower Overview

Common garden “geraniums” (actually Pelargonium hybrids) are classic potted and bedding plants with clusters of red, pink, or white blooms. They were brought to U.S. gardens from South Africa centuries ago and remain popular for their heat tolerance and the citrusy, deer-resistant smell of their leaves.
