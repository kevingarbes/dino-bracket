Canterbury Bells

Canterbury bells (Campanula medium) produce showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet-blue, pink, or white that earned them their churchly name. Grown in English cottage gardens since at least the 1500s, they were often planted along pilgrimage routes to Canterbury. A special form called the “Cup and Saucer” Canterbury bell has an extra frilly calyx under each bell, making the blooms look like little teacups on saucers - a delightful quirk that showcases this plant’s old-fashioned charm.
