Flower Overview

Common evening primrose (Oenothera biennis) opens its yellow, sweet-scented flowers at dusk, attracting night-flying moths. By day the blooms often close or fade, lasting just one evening. This hardy wildflower’s seeds are rich in gamma-linolenic acid (GLA); Native Americans and later herbalists used evening primrose for ailments, and today its oil is a popular dietary supplement. It’s a self-sowing biennial that can pop up year after year once introduced to a site.
