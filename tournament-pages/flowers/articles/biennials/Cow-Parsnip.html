Biennials Division - #14 Seed

Cow Parsnip

Cow parsnip (Heracleum maximum) is the largest native biennial wildflower in North America, with huge umbrella-like flower clusters a foot across and leaves the size of dinner plates. It was named Heracleum after Hercules for its giant stature. Indigenous peoples ate the young stems in small quantities (after careful preparation to remove acrid sap) and used the hollow stalks as flutes. Hikers admire its majestic blooms, but caution: like its relative giant hogweed, cow parsnip’s sap can cause skin irritation in sunlight, a trait that reminds one that even giants need respect.
