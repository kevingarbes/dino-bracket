Biennials Division - #16 Seed

Iceland Poppy

Iceland poppies (Papaver nudicaule) are short-lived perennials often grown as biennials, valued for their crinkled, silky petals in sherbet shades of orange, peach, yellow, and white. Native to subarctic regions, they thrive in cool summers; American gardeners often sow them one year for a gorgeous display the next spring. These poppies were once a staple in Arctic expeditions’ diets - not as food, but as a quick source of vitamin C, since the petals are surprisingly high in the vitamin and were brewed into teas to prevent scurvy.
