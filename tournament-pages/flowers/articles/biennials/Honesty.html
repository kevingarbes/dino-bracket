Biennials Division - #8 Seed

Honesty

Honesty (Lunaria annua), also known as the money plant, bears purple or white flowers in spring of its second year, but it’s most famous for what comes after: flat silvery seed pods that resemble coins. These papery translucent discs have symbolized transparency and sincerity (hence “honesty”) in folklore. Often used in dried arrangements, the seed pods remain attractive through winter and rattle in the wind; in fact, English settlers brought money plant to America, and it now grows wild in many regions, lending “coins” to the roadside flora.
