Hollyhock

Hollyhocks are old cottage-garden favorites that grow towering spikes (6–8 feet tall) of large crêpe-textured blooms in their second year. Heirloom varieties are so enduring that they often self-sow around old homesteads. In fact, hollyhocks were once nicknamed “outhouse flowers” - pioneers planted them to hide outhouses, and a stand of tall hollyhocks was a subtle signal of where the privy was located.

Flower Overview

Hollyhocks are old cottage-garden favorites that grow towering spikes (6–8 feet tall) of large crêpe-textured blooms in their second year.
