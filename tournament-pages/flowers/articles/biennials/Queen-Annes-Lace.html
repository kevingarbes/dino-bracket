Queen Anne’s Lace

Queen Anne’s lace (Daucus carota) is the wild biennial form of the carrot, with lacy white flower umbels that bloom in the second year. Folklore claims the name honors Queen Anne of England, who pricked her finger while tatting lace—a drop of her blood is said to be the tiny red floret often seen at the flower’s center. Interestingly, the familiar orange carrot was bred from this wild ancestor; if left unharvested, a garden carrot will eventually produce its own Queen Anne’s lace bloom, completing its biennial cycle.
