Flower Overview

Common foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) sends up tall spires of bell-shaped purple or white flowers in its second year, then dies after seeding. Despite its beauty, the entire plant is poisonous - however, in small doses the cardiac glycosides in foxglove leaves are a potent medicine (the source of the drug digitalis, used to strengthen heart contractions?). The name “foxglove” comes from folklore that foxes wore the blossoms on their paws like gloves to silence their footsteps while hunting.
