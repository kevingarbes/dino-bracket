Mullein

Common mullein (Verbascum thapsus) forms a rosette of fuzzy, silver-gray leaves its first year and in the second year shoots up a tall spike (up to 8 feet) studded with small yellow flowers. Early American settlers nicknamed it “flannel leaf” for its soft, woolly foliage, using the leaves in folk remedies for coughs and congestion. Resourceful pioneers also dipped dried mullein flower stalks in tallow to make temporary torches or “candlewick” - giving this adaptable biennial another old name: candlewick plant.
