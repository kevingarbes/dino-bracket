Sweet Rocket

Dame’s rocket (Hesperis matronalis) is a fragrant biennial wildflower that produces phlox-like clusters of purple, pink, or white blooms in late spring of its second year. Brought from Europe in the 17th century (Thomas Jefferson grew it at Monticello), it has since naturalized across North America. The name “dame’s rocket” reflects its swift growth (rocket) and its popularity in ladies’ ornamental gardens (dame’s). Its evening scent intensifies, perfuming the air—however, in some areas it’s considered invasive, so it’s both admired and cautiously managed.
