Biennials Division - #12 Seed

Teasel

Common teasel (Dipsacus fullonum) is a tall biennial weed with prickly stems and cone-shaped flower heads packed with tiny lavender blooms. In its second year, teasel’s dried seed heads have hooked bracts that were once used to card wool, acting like natural combs (the word “teasel” stems from Old English t?sel, meaning “to tease [cloth]”). While teasel can be invasive, its architecturally interesting seed heads are beloved by crafters for dried arrangements and by goldfinches that feast on its abundant seeds in winter.
