Sweet William

Sweet William (Dianthus barbatus) forms low clusters of fringed flowers in clove-like scents and rich colors like magenta, red, and white. Often grown alongside its perennial carnation cousins, this biennial was cultivated in colonial American gardens. Its name’s origin is debated: some say it honors Prince William, Duke of Cumberland, in the 18th century while others romantically link it to William Shakespeare?. Either way, these “pinks” add old-fashioned charm and are excellent for cutting.
