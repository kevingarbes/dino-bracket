Wallflower

English wallflower (Erysimum cheiri) is a biennial flower that, true to its name, can grow in the crevices of old stone walls. It has been a staple of spring gardens for centuries, valued for its richly sweet fragrance and warm colors of yellow, orange, and russet. The plant’s tendency to hug walls at medieval dances led to the term “wallflower” for a person who remains at the sidelines. In the 19th century, Victorian gardeners also prized wallflowers as one of the first blooms of spring, often planting them in autumn so they’d overwinter and flower abundantly come April.

Flower Overview

English wallflower (Erysimum cheiri) is a biennial flower that, true to its name, can grow in the crevices of old stone walls. It has been a staple of spring gardens for centuries, valued for its richly sweet fragrance and warm colors of yellow, orange, and russet. The plant’s tendency to hug walls at medieval dances led to the term “wallflower” for a person who remains at the sidelines. In the 19th century, Victorian gardeners also prized wallflowers as one of the first blooms of spring, often planting them in autumn so they’d overwinter and flower abundantly come April.
